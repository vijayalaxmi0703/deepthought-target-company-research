DeepThought Target Company Research
Objective:
This project was done as part of the DeepThought Business Analytics Internship assignment.
The goal was to identify 25 high-quality “Federer companies” — Indian specialty manufacturing MSMEs that fit DeepThought’s ideal customer profile. These are companies in the ₹50–₹500 Cr revenue range that are actively growing, technically strong, and building in-house capabilities.

What is a “Federer Company”?
In simple terms, a Federer company is:
A growing, technically-driven manufacturing MSME where the founder or leadership is focused on building capability inside the company rather than outsourcing it.
These companies usually:
Operate in specialty or technical manufacturing segments
Have real production facilities (not just trading or services)
Show signs of growth like hiring, expansion, or certifications
Are led by technically strong founders or engineers/scientists
Are open to structured ways of improving operations and growth
How I approached the research
I followed a step-by-step process instead of just collecting random company names:

1. Building the initial list
I first collected a broad set of companies using:
MCA database
LinkedIn company search
Industry association lists (like CII, Pharmexcil, etc.)
Exhibitor lists from industrial expos
Google searches for manufacturers in specific sectors
This gave a large raw list of companies across different manufacturing segments.

3. Filtering
Next, I filtered out companies that clearly didn’t fit:
Removed service companies, traders, and distributors
Excluded CROs and testing labs (even if they looked relevant)
Removed very large conglomerates and PE-owned companies
Focused only on specialty manufacturing in the ₹50–₹500 Cr range

4. Scoring companies (C1–C6 framework)
Each company was then evaluated based on:
Whether they actually manufacture in-house
How technically differentiated their product is
Background of founder/leadership (technical vs non-technical)
Whether the sector is growing (export demand, government push, etc.)
Visible growth signals like hiring, expansion, certifications, etc.
This helped separate strong matches from weak or irrelevant ones.

5. Final validation
For the shortlisted companies, I manually checked:
Company websites
Product details
Certifications (ISO, GMP, USFDA, etc.)
Hiring activity and recent updates
This step helped remove false positives and improve quality.

Scoring system:
Each company was scored out of 100 using a simple weighted system:
Manufacturing strength
India presence
Technical differentiation
Founder/decision-maker background
Sector growth potential
Active growth signals
Based on total score:
80+ → Strong Federer fit
60–79 → Good fit
40–59 → Borderline
Below 40 → Excluded

What’s included in this repo:

Each company entry includes:
What the company does
Estimated revenue range

A personalization hook that could be used for outreach

Key takeaway from the research:
Most companies initially looked relevant but were filtered out because they were:
Not true manufacturers
Too generic or commodity-based
Lacking any real growth signals
Subsidiaries or institutionally controlled
Only a small percentage actually fit the Federer profile after proper validation.
